❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What Is BMI & How Is It Calculated?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a widely used screening tool that estimates body fat based on height and weight. Developed by Adolphe Quetelet in the 19th century, it remains a key metric used by the WHO, CDC, and healthcare providers globally.

The BMI Formula

Metric: BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height² (m²)  |  Imperial: BMI = (weight lbs × 703) ÷ height² (in²)

BMI Limitations

BMI doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, ethnicity, age, or sex. Athletes may show a high BMI despite low body fat. Use it alongside other indicators and consult a healthcare professional for a complete assessment.

  • Athletes may show high BMI despite low body fat due to muscle mass
  • Older adults may have normal BMI but excess body fat (sarcopenic obesity)
  • Asian populations face health risks at lower BMI thresholds (overweight ≥23)
  • Pregnant women should not use standard BMI classifications
