How Much Water Do You Really Need?

The famous "8 glasses a day" rule is a myth — it has no scientific basis. Your actual daily water needs depend on your body weight, physical activity, climate, health status, and diet. The Institute of Medicine (IOM) recommends a more personalised approach.

The Science: How This Calculator Works

This calculator uses the weight-based formula endorsed by most nutrition experts:

💧 Base formula: 35 ml per kg of body weight per day
+ Activity adjustment (0–1,200 ml depending on exercise intensity)
+ Climate adjustment (up to +500 ml in hot/humid conditions)

Signs You're Dehydrated

  • Dark yellow urine (pale yellow = well hydrated)
  • Feeling thirsty (thirst is a late sign of dehydration)
  • Dry mouth or lips, headache, difficulty concentrating
  • Fatigue, dizziness, or decreased urine output
✅ Quick check: Your urine colour is the simplest hydration indicator. Aim for pale lemon-yellow. Dark yellow = drink more. Colourless = you may be overhydrated.
