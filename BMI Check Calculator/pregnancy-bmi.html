Pregnancy Weight Gain Guidelines (IOM 2009)

The Institute of Medicine (IOM) recommends different gestational weight gain ranges based on your pre-pregnancy BMI. These guidelines are designed to support optimal outcomes for both mother and baby.

Pre-Pregnancy BMICategoryRecommended Total Gain2nd & 3rd Trimester (per week)
< 18.5Underweight12.5 – 18 kg (28–40 lbs)0.51 kg (1.0–1.3 lbs)
18.5 – 24.9Normal Weight11.5 – 16 kg (25–35 lbs)0.42 kg (0.8–1.0 lbs)
25.0 – 29.9Overweight7 – 11.5 kg (15–25 lbs)0.28 kg (0.5–0.7 lbs)
≥ 30.0Obese5 – 9 kg (11–20 lbs)0.22 kg (0.4–0.6 lbs)
⚠️ Note: These are guidelines for singleton pregnancies. Twin pregnancies require greater weight gain. Always follow your healthcare provider's personalized recommendations.

Why Pre-Pregnancy BMI Matters

Your pre-pregnancy BMI helps healthcare providers assess risks and tailor advice throughout your pregnancy. Being underweight increases risks of preterm birth and low birth weight. Being overweight or obese increases risks of gestational diabetes, hypertension, and caesarean delivery.

BMI During Pregnancy — Special Considerations

Standard BMI is not used to classify weight status during pregnancy itself, since weight gain is expected and normal. Instead, healthcare providers track weight gain trajectory against IOM recommendations for your pre-pregnancy BMI category. The goal is steady, appropriate gain — not restriction or excess.
